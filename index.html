Trading Strategies

Breakout Strategy

Learn how to identify and trade breakouts effectively.

This strategy works by identifying stocks that have been consolidating and then breaking out of their trading range. A sudden increase in volume confirms the breakout, making it a prime opportunity to trade. The breakout strategy works best in stocks with strong momentum and clear resistance levels.

  • Step 1: Identify consolidation range
  • Step 2: Wait for volume spike
  • Step 3: Enter trade at breakout point

Trend Following

Ride the trend and maximize profits.

Trend-following strategies involve analyzing price movement and identifying an ongoing trend. Traders aim to enter the market in the direction of the trend, usually after confirming it using technical indicators like moving averages or trendlines. The key to this strategy is patience and discipline.

  • Step 1: Identify trend direction
  • Step 2: Use moving averages or trendlines
  • Step 3: Enter trade with proper risk management

Scalping

Short-term trading techniques for active traders.

Scalping involves executing multiple trades within a short time frame, usually holding positions for just a few minutes. This strategy requires strict risk management, as profits per trade are small. Scalpers usually trade on very liquid stocks and often make dozens of trades per day.

  • Step 1: Identify liquid stocks with high volume
  • Step 2: Execute multiple trades for small profits
  • Step 3: Use tight stop-loss and risk management
